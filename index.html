The Cherwell Quartet

About Us

We are a student-based string quartet, and play a variety of different genres of music for occasions such as weddings, balls, birthday parties, and drinks receptions. The Cherwell Quartet is made up of a team of young musicians, largely based at the University of Oxford. We are available to play for your event and have been working in the Oxford area for the past 3 years.

Helen Leung

Violin/Viola & Founder

Helen studied musical performance between the ages of 10-18 at the Primary Royal Academy of Music, and the Royal College of Music Junior Department in London. She plays the piano (DipABRSM, LRSM), violin, viola (LTCL), and organ, and has played with the Oxford University Orchestra during her time at Oxford.

Poppy Miller

Cello

Poppy most recently studied the cello (DipABRSM) with Alexander Boyarsky and is also a keen pianist. She attended the Royal College of Music Junior Department in London as a teenager and is now principal cellist of the Oxford University Orchestra.

Katalin Oldland

Violin

Katalin studied the violin (DipABRSM) at the Royal College of Music Junior Department from the age of 9 and is now a keen performer and music student at Oxford. Most recently she had led the Oxford University Orchestra, the Oxford University Sinfonietta and the New College Opera.

Emily Boyle

Violin

Emily studied the violin and viola with Garfield Jackson and is a former member of the National Children’s Orchestras of Great Britain. She currently plays in the Oxford University Orchestra and Oxford University Sinfonietta and has previously performed with New College Opera.

Phoebe Tealby-Watson

Violin

Phoebe’s first study is the voice, currently singing with The Oxford Gargoyles and the National Youth Choir, having previously been a choral scholar. She studied violin between the ages of 8-18, achieving a distinction in her grade 8, before starting to teach younger students. She has received masterclass tuition from Nicola Benedetti, and has previously worked with Britten Sinfonia and Saffron Walden Music Academy.
